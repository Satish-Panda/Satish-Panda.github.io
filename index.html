About Me

I am a final-year undergraduate at the Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur double majoring in EE and CSE. I do my undergraduate research in algorithms and distributed planning models. I am a 2025 IUSSTF-Viterbi Scholar and currently interning at the Department of CS, Viterbi School of Engineering, USC.

Research Interests

My research interest is in Algorithms, Game Theory and Probabilistic ML. Their applications in Online Learning, Distributed Planning and Computational Social Choice interest me. I idealise clean models with theoretical guarantees and practical implications.

ML, Algorithm AND Theory Background

Fascinated by the theory underlying machine learning, I took graduate level courses in both classical and probabilistic Machine Learning. In my course project on Federated learning, I explored methods for better aggregating client to global server communications and tried a low rank Laplace approximation instead of the usual diagonal one to see if it is better to compromise dimensionality for better cross covariance representation of top k components.

After doing a course in data structures and algorithms, I took an advanced course on design and analysis of algorithms. I also did the undergraduate course in theory of computation. Thereafter, I took a graduate level course in Algorithmic game theory. I loved the parts on auctions and mechanism design. In that course, as a reading component, I chose to do a study of Fair allocation of Indivisible items under externalities.
