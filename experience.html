IUSSTF-Viterbi Research Program, University of Southern California (May 2025 - Present)

I have been interning with Professor Evi Micha at the Viterbi School of Engineering, USC starting this May where I spent the summer on the USC campus. The manuscript of our work in Proportional Social Fairness in Clustering is in progress. Proportional Social Fairness in clustering is a setting where any group of agents of size of at least a specified minimum feels that the clustering is fair to the them within a certain tolerance.

Different definitions of fairness notions have given rise to sub domains within Proportionally Fair Clustering. Offline Solutions for Fair Classical and Transferable Centroid clustering and Fair Classical Non Centroid setting have been well studied previously. I showed the impossibility of clustering guarantee in the proposed new setting of Transferable Non Centroid Clustering. Since then, we study online algorithms with single and double recourse and have obtained some nice results showing constant order online approximation of offline solutions.

Distributed Planning Models, Depatment of CSE, Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur (September 2025 - Present)

Advised by Professor Sunil Simon, Department of Computer Science and Engineering, we work on distributed planning models. In the distributed setting, agents have individual objective specifications and a set of personal propositions that they control. The agents are unaware of the truth values of others' propositions only having partial view of the global state. The agents communicate among themselves to exchange proposition values to achieve their objectives.

The communication actions between agents are restricted to broadcast because general communication in appears to make the objective undecidable. The communication actions are modelled through epistemic modalities. The objective specification language augmented with epistemic operators for multi-agent environments borrows from the SPRECTRL specification language, a restriction of LTL known to be decidable.

Internship at Texas Instrument, Bangalore, India (May 2024 - July 2024)

I interned at Texas Instruments, Bangalore, India in the summer of 2024. I designed a memory addressing scheme for Fast Fourier Transform algorithms with re-configurability and parallelization. The key was identifying the inherent pattern of stagewise change of element indexing and tuple formation underlying all Fast Fourier Algorithms (including multi-radix). For this work, I was offered a novel full-time role in optimized micro-architecture design for algorithms by leveraging their innate properties.

I devised an addressing scheme using just addition and modulo operations in a mixed base indexing scheme that not only satisfied re-configurability by accepting any factor of the max-allowable input length as inputs but also did the computation in-place. Unlike previous industry implementations, the proposed scheme requires only a single memory block of size equal to max input length.
